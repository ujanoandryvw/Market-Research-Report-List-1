<p><p>The Flexible Lithium-Ion Batteries market encompasses various components essential for battery functionality. The positive pole, typically made from lithium metal oxides, facilitates the flow of lithium ions during discharge. The negative pole, often using graphite, stores lithium ions during charging. The diaphragm serves as a separator, preventing short circuits while allowing ion passage. The electrolyte, a conductive medium, enables ion movement between the electrodes. Together, these components contribute to the development of lightweight, adaptable battery solutions for diverse applications.</p></p>
