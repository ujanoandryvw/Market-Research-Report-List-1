<p><p>The zero-calorie sweetener market comprises several key types, each with distinct properties. Sucralose is known for its heat stability and suitability in baking. Aspartame offers a sugar-like taste but is unstable at high temperatures. Saccharin, one of the oldest sweeteners, is widely used in processed foods. Cyclamate is often banned in some countries but remains popular elsewhere. Stevia, a natural sweetener derived from plants, has gained traction for its health appeal. Ace-K and Neotame are synthetic options used in various food and beverage applications, providing intense sweetness with minimal calories.</p></p>
