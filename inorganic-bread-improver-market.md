<p><p>The inorganic bread improver market comprises several types, including emulsifiers, enzymes, oxidizing agents, reducing agents, and others. Emulsifiers enhance dough stability and texture. Enzymes improve fermentation and shelf life by breaking down starches and proteins. Oxidizing agents strengthen gluten structure, contributing to better volume and crumb texture. Reducing agents work by softening the dough, enhancing extensibility. "Others" may include various additives that support dough management and improve overall bread quality, catering to diverse baking needs.</p></p>
