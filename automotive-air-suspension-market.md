<p><p>The automotive air suspension market is divided into two main types: non-electronically controlled and electronically controlled systems. Non-electronically controlled air suspension relies on basic mechanical components to adjust air pressure in the airbags, offering a simpler, cost-effective solution primarily in older vehicles. In contrast, electronically controlled air suspension utilizes advanced sensors and control units to dynamically adjust the suspension settings, providing enhanced ride comfort, stability, and improved handling in various driving conditions, making it popular in luxury and high-performance vehicles.</p></p>
