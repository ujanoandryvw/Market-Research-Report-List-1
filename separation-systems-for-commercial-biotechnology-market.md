<p><p>Separation systems in commercial biotechnology encompass various techniques for isolating and purifying biological substances. Microarrays enable simultaneous analysis of multiple samples. Lab-on-a-chip integrates multiple laboratory functions onto a single chip for efficient analysis. Biochips serve similar purposes in high-throughput screening. Magnetic separation utilizes magnetic fields to isolate cells or biomolecules. Chromatography separates components based on interactions with stationary phases, while flow cytometry analyzes cell populations. Membrane filtration sieves particles through porous membranes, electrophoresis separates charged molecules by size, and centrifugation uses centrifugal force for separation. Other methods encompass emerging and diverse techniques tailored to specific applications.</p></p>
